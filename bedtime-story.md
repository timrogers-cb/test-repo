# The Little Star Who Couldn't Sleep

Once upon a time, high above the soft hills and sleepy meadows, there lived a little star named Lumie.

Every night, when the moon rose and the world below grew quiet, all the other stars would blink gently and drift into a peaceful glow. But Lumie would twinkle and fidget, spinning in tiny circles across the sky.

"I can't sleep!" Lumie sighed, sending a small shower of sparkles tumbling down toward the earth.

The Moon, who was very wise and very round, drifted over slowly. "What is the matter, little Lumie?" she asked in her soft, silver voice.

"Every time I close my eyes," said Lumie, "I think of all the things I haven't seen yet — the mountains, the oceans, the children in their beds!"

The Moon smiled warmly. "Ah," she said, "but that is precisely why you must rest. For tomorrow night, you will shine your brightest, and all those things will be there waiting for you to light them up."

Lumie thought about this for a moment. Far below, a child was looking up through a bedroom window, watching the stars. The child yawned, snuggled deeper under their blanket, and whispered, "Good night, stars."

And just like that, something warm and gentle settled inside Lumie. The little star stopped spinning.

Lumie looked out at the great, quiet sky — at the sleeping clouds, the dreaming mountains, and all the cozy little houses tucked into the darkness — and felt, for the first time, perfectly still.

"Good night, world," Lumie whispered.

And with a last soft twinkle, the little star closed its eyes.

*The end. Sweet dreams.* 🌟
